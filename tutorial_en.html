The Scanners (What is being checked?)
S1

Sender Check (Phishing Scanner)

Checks if the sender is authentic.

Important Warnings:
  • Sender Spoofing
  • Incorrect Reply-To addresses
Simply explained:
This scanner looks under the hood of the email. It checks whether the address where a reply would be sent actually belongs to the sender. If differences are found, it is a strong indicator of fraud.
Click for details
S2

Link Check (URL Scanner)

Checks all web links in the email.

Important Warnings:
  • Hidden destinations (Shorteners)
  • Deceptively authentic names
  • Malicious website lists
Simply explained:
Scammers often use tricks to make links look harmless. The scanner detects if links have been shortened to hide the destination or if characters were swapped (e.g., 'rn' instead of 'm') to mimic well-known company names.
Click for details
S3

Text Check (Readability Scanner)

Checks how the email is written.

Important Warnings:
  • Extremely short texts
  • Very simple sentences
Simply explained:
Many phishing emails are generated automatically or poorly translated. If an email is conspicuously short or consists of extremely simple, choppy sentences, this scanner sounds the alarm.
Click for details
S5

Account Check (IBAN Scanner)

Checks included bank details.

Important Warnings:
  • IBAN detected
  • Bank's country of origin
Simply explained:
Payment requests are typical for fraud. The tool finds account numbers (IBANs) in the text and reminds you to check them carefully. It also checks if the bank's country matches the alleged sender.
Click for details
S6

Word Check (Keyword Scanner)

Searches for typical scam keywords.

Important Warnings:
  • Words like: Invoice, Blocked, Password, Login
Simply explained:
This scanner looks for words often used to create pressure or fear. Terms like "Verify immediately", "Account blocked", or "Payment due" lead to a higher risk assessment.
